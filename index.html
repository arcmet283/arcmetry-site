Data Credibility

What ARCmetry uses to build your report

ARCmetry reports are built from labor market and credential data sources that help identify realistic wage outcomes, pathway viability, and training routes.

Data behind the analysis

ARCmetry combines labor market wage data, state-level workforce information, credential pathway research, and practical barrier screening to identify which options are realistic, fundable, and likely to improve income.

  • Bureau of Labor Statistics — occupational wages, outlook, and role benchmarks
  • State workforce data — regional demand patterns, approved training, and placement infrastructure
  • Regional labor market trends — what pays in your area, not just nationally
  • Credential program pathways — training length, stackability, and entry requirements

What that means for you

Instead of generic career inspiration, you receive a report that answers practical questions: what role fits, what it pays, what credential gets you there fastest, what barriers matter, and what steps to take first.

  • Pathway fit — matched to your work history, goals, and budget
  • Wage visibility — projected income at different career stages
  • Training clarity — what you need, how long it takes, and what it may cost
  • Execution plan — the first actions to move you forward quickly

FAQ

Common Questions

How long does the report take?
Reports are typically delivered within 48–72 hours after your completed intake form is submitted.
What data does ARCmetry use?
ARCmetry uses labor market data sources such as Bureau of Labor Statistics information, state workforce data, regional labor market trends, and credential pathway research.
Who is this service designed for?
ARCmetry is designed for people who want a faster, clearer path to higher-paying work — whether they are exploring, moving up, or planning a major career shift.
How do I know which report to choose?
Choose Pathway Analysis if you need new-career direction, Mobility Analysis if you want faster income growth using your existing skills, and Career Strategy if you are making a higher-level career move.
Do I need a resume to get started?
No. A resume is helpful, but not required. The intake form is designed to collect the information needed to build your report.
What format is the report delivered in?
Reports are delivered as a professionally formatted PDF to your email inbox.
